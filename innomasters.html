Innomasters Engineering Design Program

A six-week engineering design program for high school students hosted by Engineers Without Borders UofT. I helped develop the instructional framework, workshop flow, student activities, collaboration resources, and final showcase evaluation system.

← All projectsResume

Program Overview

Innomasters introduced high school students to engineering design through interactive workshops, real-world humanitarian design prompts, guided team activities, and a final engineering showcase. The program was structured to help students move from problem understanding to concept development and presentation.

My Role

  • Developed workshop slides and student-facing learning activities.
  • Created worksheets to guide problem analysis and concept generation.
  • Built collaboration resources such as team charter prompts.
  • Helped design showcase expectations and evaluation criteria.
  • Supported mentors by making workshop materials easier to facilitate.

Workshop Leadership

What I helped develop

Workshop 1

Foundations of Meaningful Design

Built introductory content to explain what engineering is, why design starts with real human problems, and how engineers use a structured process to turn needs into solutions.

  • Introduced the engineering design process: research, requirements, idea generation, and final design.
  • Explained research quality using the CRAAP framework.
  • Created examples for problem statements, gap, need, and scope.
  • Included an interactive activity to practice breaking down a design problem.
Workshop 2

Team Formation & Engineering Requirements

Helped structure the session around team formation, collaboration expectations, and core requirement concepts used in engineering design.

  • Designed the team charter activity so students could define roles, expectations, communication methods, and conflict-resolution plans.
  • Introduced the natural disaster design challenge used throughout the program.
  • Explained stakeholders, service environment, functions, objectives, and constraints.
  • Connected engineering requirements to real-world disaster preparedness problems.
Workshop 3

Problem Analysis Framework

Created guided worksheet content that helped students analyze the problem before jumping to solutions.

  • Guided students through stakeholder analysis and direct/indirect impact mapping.
  • Helped students define the current state, desired state, and engineering gap.
  • Developed sections for need identification, scope boundaries, service environment, objectives, and constraints.
  • Encouraged solution-independent thinking so teams could avoid locking into one idea too early.
Workshop 4

Idea Generation & Concept Design

Built content and worksheet structure for brainstorming, comparing multiple ideas, and selecting a final concept.

  • Explained divergent thinking and why quantity matters during early ideation.
  • Designed activities around free brainstorming, structured brainstorming, and morph charts.
  • Created a concept comparison table using criteria such as safety, cost, durability, ease of use, and effectiveness.
  • Prompted teams to improve their chosen design instead of stopping at the first feasible idea.
Workshop 5

Showcase Preparation & Engineering Communication

Prepared students for the final showcase by teaching how to communicate engineering decisions clearly.

  • Created presentation guidance focused on problem, design, requirements, and why the solution works.
  • Emphasized simple slides, visuals, clear speaking, and professional presentation habits.
  • Outlined the expected presentation structure and final preparation timeline.
  • Helped teams connect design choices back to functions, constraints, and objectives.
Workshop 6

Final Showcase & Evaluation

Helped define the evaluation framework used for final student presentations.

  • Created rubric criteria for problem understanding, design idea, feasibility, clarity of explanation, and team participation.
  • Helped standardize expectations so judges and students had a consistent evaluation framework.
  • Supported the final showcase structure where teams presented their design solutions and received completion certificates.
