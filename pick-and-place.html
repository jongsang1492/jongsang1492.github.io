Overview

Conceptual design of a compact automated PCB assembly machine.

The project focused on a desktop-sized machine that could place electrical components onto PCBs using a moving nozzle system, fixed component reels, and a gantry-style layout. The design prioritized compatibility, precision, usability, and manufacturability over industrial-scale speed.

The final design included XY belt-driven motion, rack-and-pinion Z-axis motion, dual pneumatic nozzle modules, adjustable PCB retention slides, reel feeders, camera modules, and a rigid base/frame structure.

Z-Axis Design Focus

Rack-and-pinion motion module

The Z-axis subsystem was designed to provide controlled vertical motion for the nozzle assembly. The mechanism uses a rack-and-pinion layout supported by guide elements to maintain alignment while integrating with the larger gantry assembly.

  • Compact motor-driven vertical motion
  • Dual nozzle packaging and mounting
  • Guide-supported rack movement
  • Modular assembly drawings for manufacturability
