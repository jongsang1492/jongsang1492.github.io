Custom Current-Sensing Power Supply

Designed, fabricated, soldered, and validated a custom current-sense PCB for a programmable DC power supply. The project combined LM324-based analog signal conditioning, two-layer PCB layout in Autodesk Eagle, through-hole assembly, measurement validation, and integration with an LCD-based embedded power supply platform.

System Context

Programmable DC power supply integration

The current-sense PCB converts the voltage across a low-value current-sense resistor into a microcontroller-readable signal. It sits inside a larger programmable power supply system with a laptop adapter input, switching regulator, microcontroller, LCD/pushbutton interface, and output terminals.

Analog circuit design

Built an LM324N-based current-sense amplifier and active filtering circuit to condition the measured current signal for the embedded controller.

PCB layout

Created a compact two-layer PCB in Autodesk Eagle, routing top and bottom copper layers around the LM324N, feedback networks, connector header, and through-hole components.

Testing & validation

Measured VOUT1 and VOUT2 across the tested current range, plotted ADC trends, and validated that both filtered and unfiltered outputs followed a clean increasing response.

Circuit Architecture

LM324N signal conditioning and filter stages

The schematic uses an LM324N quad op-amp package to condition the ISENSE signal. The design includes an input current-sense stage, amplification and biasing around VOUT1, additional active low-pass filtering, and a connector interface for VCC, ISENSE, VOUT1, VOUT2, and GND.

  • Current-sense input routed from ISENSE and 0.1 Ω sense resistor path
  • LM324N stages used for analog amplification, buffering, and filtering
  • VOUT1 and VOUT2 separated for unfiltered and filtered measurements
  • JP1 header exposes power, signal, and ground connections for integration
