Robotic Arm Design and Analysis

Ongoing mechanical design and structural analysis project for a 3-DOF robotic arm and gripper system. The project focuses on CAD assembly, joint design, material selection, and SolidWorks Simulation validation under external loading.

Started Apr 2026Currently in developmentSolidWorksFEAMechanical Design

Project Overview

Designing the arm first, then using simulation to expose weak points.

This project focuses on the mechanical design and structural analysis of a 3-DOF robotic arm and gripper system using SolidWorks and SolidWorks Simulation. The goal is to evaluate whether the arm links can support external loads and to identify stress-critical regions before physical manufacturing.

The current version includes a complete CAD assembly, link-level static structural simulations, and early design validation. The project is still being developed with future improvements planned for dynamic motion, motor integration, and physical prototyping.

Mechanical Design

3-DOF arm with gripper and base-mounted assembly.

The robotic arm consists of multiple interconnected links and a gripper mechanism. The design was developed with manufacturability, assembly simplicity, and structural performance in mind.

  • Joint connectivity and motion constraints
  • Structural stability under loading
  • Manufacturability of individual components
  • Weight and material selection
  • Ease of assembly and future actuator integration

FEA Setup

Static structural analysis of critical links

MaterialAluminum 1060 used for the current simulation baseline
Applied Loading1.6 N·m torque used to represent an estimated 1 kg payload case at arm reach
ToolSolidWorks Simulation static structural FEA
PurposeFind stress concentrations, compare peak stress to yield strength, and guide redesign decisions

Link 1 Analysis

Stress slightly exceeded yield under the tested load case.

Link 1 was evaluated under a 1.6 N·m applied torque. The maximum von Mises stress reached 28.86 MPa compared with a 27.57 MPa yield strength, giving an estimated factor of safety of 0.96.

  • Maximum von Mises Stress: 28.86 MPa
  • Yield Strength: 27.57 MPa
  • Estimated Factor of Safety: 0.96
  • Result: requires geometry/material optimization before manufacturing

Link 3 Analysis

Stress remained below yield for the current simulation condition.

Link 3 was also evaluated under a 1.6 N·m applied torque. The peak von Mises stress reached 25.01 MPa, remaining below the 27.57 MPa yield strength with an estimated factor of safety of 1.10.

  • Maximum von Mises Stress: 25.01 MPa
  • Yield Strength: 27.57 MPa
  • Estimated Factor of Safety: 1.10
  • Result: passes the current static load case, but still marked for future reinforcement review
